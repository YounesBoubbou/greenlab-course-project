 Here is a list of the capitals for most European countries, with some exceptions:

1. Albania - Tirana
2. Andorra - Andorra la Vella
3. Austria - Vienna (Wien)
4. Belarus - Minsk
5. Belgium - Brussels (Brussel in Flemish, Bruxelles in French)
6. Bosnia and Herzegovina - Sarajevo
7. Bulgaria - Sofia
8. Croatia - Zagreb
9. Czech Republic - Prague (Praha)
10. Denmark - Copenhagen (København)
11. Estonia - Tallinn
12. Finland - Helsinki (Helsingfors)
13. France - Paris
14. Georgia - Tbilisi
15. Germany - Berlin
16. Greece - Athens (Athina)
17. Hungary - Budapest
18. Iceland - Reykjavik
19. Ireland - Dublin
20. Italy - Rome (Roma)
21. Kosovo (recognized by some countries, not all) - Pristina
22. Latvia - Riga
23. Liechtenstein - Vaduz
24. Lithuania - Vilnius
25. Luxembourg - Luxembourg City (Luxembourg-Stadt in German, Ville de Luxembourg in French)
26. Macedonia - Skopje
27. Malta - Valletta
28. Monaco - Monte Carlo
29. Montenegro - Podgorica
30. Netherlands - Amsterdam
31. Norway - Oslo
32. Poland - Warsaw (Warszawa)
33. Portugal - Lisbon (Lisboa)
34. Romania - Bucharest (București)
35. Russia - Moscow (Moskva)
36. San Marino - City of San Marino (Città di San Marino)
37. Serbia - Belgrade (Beograd)
38. Slovakia - Bratislava
39. Slovenia - Ljubljana
40. Spain - Madrid
41. Sweden - Stockholm
42. Switzerland - Bern (Bern in German, Berne in French and Italian)
43. Ukraine - Kiev (Kyiv)
44. United Kingdom - London
45. Vatican City - Vatican City (Città del Vaticano)

There are two countries without a designated capital: Kazakhstan and Norway (Svalbard). Also, the Republic of Kosovo is recognized as a sovereign state by some, but not all, members of the international community.
