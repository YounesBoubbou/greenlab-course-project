 Here is a list of capital cities for most European countries, but please note that Europe includes a wide variety of political entities, so some microstates may not be included:

1. Austria - Vienna (Wien)
2. Belgium - Brussels (Brussel)
3. Bosnia and Herzegovina - Sarajevo
4. Croatia - Zagreb
5. Czech Republic - Prague (Praha)
6. Denmark - Copenhagen (København)
7. Estonia - Tallinn (Tallin)
8. Finland - Helsinki (Helsingfors)
9. France - Paris (Paris)
10. Germany - Berlin (Berlin)
11. Greece - Athens (Athina)
12. Hungary - Budapest (Budapest)
13. Iceland - Reykjavik (Reykjavíkurhreppir)
14. Ireland - Dublin (Baile Átha Cliath)
15. Italy - Rome (Roma)
16. Kosovo - Pristina (Prishtinë/Priština)
17. Latvia - Riga (Rīga)
18. Lithuania - Vilnius (Vilniaus)
19. Luxembourg - City of Luxembourg (Luxemburg-Stad)
20. North Macedonia - Skopje (Skopje)
21. Netherlands - Amsterdam (Amsterdam)
22. Norway - Oslo (Oslo)
23. Poland - Warsaw (Warszawa)
24. Portugal - Lisbon (Lisboa)
25. Romania - Bucharest (București)
26. Russia (partly in Europe) - Moscow (Moskva)
27. Serbia - Belgrade (Beograd)
28. Slovakia - Bratislava (Poznaň, Prešporok, Bratislava)
29. Slovenia - Ljubljana (Ljubljana)
30. Spain - Madrid (Madrid)
31. Sweden - Stockholm (Stockholm)
32. Switzerland - Bern (Berne)
33. Turkey (european part) - Istanbul (İstanbul)
34. Ukraine - Kiev (Kyiv)
35. United Kingdom - London (Londres)
36. Vatican City - Vatican City (Città del Vaticano)

This list does not include the capitals of some de facto independent states in Europe, like Abkhazia, Artsakh, Kosovo, Northern Cyprus, and South Ossetia, as their status is generally unrecognized internationally.
