The paper defines artificial intelligence (AI) as machine-based intelligence that enables machines to perceive their environment and take actions to achieve defined goals. AI has various subfields, including reasoning, knowledge representation, planning, learning, natural language processing, perception, and support for robotics. The long-term goal of AI is general intelligence, which allows machines to complete tasks equal to or better than humans. To reach this goal, AI researchers have adapted techniques from multiple fields, such as search, formal logic, neural networks, and statistics. The field has experienced cycles of optimism and disappointment, with a significant increase in funding and interest after 2012 due to the success of deep learning and transformer architectures. Today, AI is widely used, but its unintended consequences and risks have raised concerns about regulatory policies to ensure its safety and benefits.
