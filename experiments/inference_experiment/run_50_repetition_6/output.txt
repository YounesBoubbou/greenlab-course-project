As Europe comprises 50 countries, providing the capital of each would be quite extensive. Here is a concise list of some major and notable European capitals:

1. **United Kingdom**: London
2. **France**: Paris
3. **Germany**: Berlin
4. **Italy**: Rome
5. **Spain**: Madrid
6. **Portugal**: Lisbon
7. **Netherlands**: Amsterdam
8. **Belgium**: Brussels
9. **Sweden**: Stockholm
10. **Norway**: Oslo
11. **Finland**: Helsinki
12. **Denmark**: Copenhagen
13. **Ireland**: Dublin
14. **Austria**: Vienna
15. **Switzerland**: Bern (for the federal government, but note that its largest city is Zürich)
16. **Greece**: Athens
17. **Cyprus**: Nicosia (the capital of the Republic of Cyprus; Northern Cyprus is not recognized internationally and has a separate capital in Taç Mahalla)
18. **Croatia**: Zagreb
19. **Bosnia and Herzegovina**: Sarajevo

Eastern European capitals include:

20. **Poland**: Warsaw
21. **Ukraine**: Kyiv (Kiev)
22. **Slovakia**: Bratislava
23. **Czech Republic**: Prague
24. **Romania**: Bucharest
25. **Bulgaria**: Sofia

Southeastern European capitals include:

26. **Serbia**: Belgrade
27. **Macedonia (North Macedonia)**: Skopje
28. **Montenegro**: Podgorica

The Baltic States' capitals are:

29. **Latvia**: Riga
30. **Lithuania**: Vilnius
31. **Estonia**: Tallinn

Additionally, the capitals of the smaller countries or territories include:

- **Malta**: Valletta
- **San Marino**: City of San Marino (part of the larger Republic)
- **Vatican City**: Vatican City (The Holy See)

This list covers the capital cities of 30 out of 50 European countries. The remaining capitals are for smaller nations or autonomous regions, such as Andorra (Andorra la Vella), Monaco (Monaco-Ville), Liechtenstein (Vaduz), and others with distinct administrative structures.
