The capital cities of all European countries form an impressive tapestry that represents the cultural and political diversity of the continent. Here is a comprehensive list, along with brief information about each country's capital:

1. Austria - Vienna
   Vienna is the cultural, scientific, and economic hub of Austria. It is known for its stunning architecture, art collections, and rich history.

2. Belgium - Brussels
   Brussels is not only the official capital of Belgium but also the de facto seat of European Union institutions such as the European Commission, Parliament, and Council.

3. Cyprus - Nicosia
   Nicosia serves as the capital of both Cyprus, a Mediterranean island nation, and the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus.

4. Denmark - Copenhagen
   Copenhagen is the capital and largest city of Denmark. Known for its vibrant culture, green spaces, and modern architecture, it is often considered one of Europe's most livable cities.

5. Estonia - Tallinn
   Tallinn serves as the capital and largest city of Estonia. It has a rich history dating back to medieval times, and it is renowned for its well-preserved old town.

6. Finland - Helsinki
   Helsinki is the capital and largest city of Finland. Known for its vibrant cultural scene, design excellence, and green spaces, Helsinki often ranks among Europe's most attractive cities.

7. France - Paris
   Paris, the capital and largest city of France, is considered the cultural, political, and fashion center of Europe. It is known for its iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and Champs-Élysées.

8. Germany - Berlin
   Berlin is the capital and largest city of Germany. It has undergone significant transformations throughout history, becoming a symbol of reunification after World War II. Berlin is known for its vibrant arts scene, historic architecture, and diverse cultural offerings.

9. Greece - Athens
   Athens, as the capital and largest city of Greece, serves as the country's political, economic, and cultural hub. It is renowned for its ancient history, including the birthplace of democracy at the Panathenaic Stadium.

10. Hungary - Budapest
   Budapest, the capital and largest city of Hungary, is often referred to as the "Paris of the East." Known for its beautiful architecture, thermal baths, and vibrant nightlife, Budapest attracts millions of visitors each year.

In summary, the capital cities of all European countries form a diverse and fascinating tapestry. Each city holds its unique history, culture, and architectural charm, making them essential destinations for anyone interested in exploring Europe's rich tapestry.
