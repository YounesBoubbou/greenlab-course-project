There are 47 sovereign nations that make up Europe, and each has its own capital city. The capital cities are diverse in terms of architecture, culture, and history. Some of the most famous capital cities in Europe include:

1. London, United Kingdom - known for its iconic landmarks such as Buckingham Palace, the Tower of London, and Westminster Abbey.
2. Berlin, Germany - a vibrant city with a rich history, including the Brandenburg Gate, the Reichstag building, and the East Side Gallery.
3. Paris, France - known for its romantic atmosphere, the Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Louvre Museum.
4. Madrid, Spain - home to numerous world-renowned museums, including the Prado Museum, and famous landmarks such as the Royal Palace of Madrid and the Plaza Mayor.
5. Rome, Italy - a city steeped in history and art, with iconic landmarks such as the Colosseum, the Pantheon, and St. Peter's Basilica.
6. Athens, Greece - home to numerous ancient ruins and historical landmarks, including the Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Temple of Olympian Zeus.
7. Vienna, Austria - known for its rich cultural heritage, including famous composers such as Mozart and Beethoven, and iconic landmarks such as St. Stephen's Cathedral and the Hofburg Palace.
8. Brussels, Belgium - the capital city of Belgium, home to the European Union and numerous world-renowned museums, including the Atomium and the Mini-Europe.
9. Amsterdam, Netherlands - a bustling city with a rich history, including the Van Gogh Museum, the Rijksmuseum, and iconic landmarks such as the Anne Frank House and the Red Light District.
10. Stockholm, Sweden - known for its beautiful architecture, including the Royal Palace of Stockholm and Gamla Stan, and its vibrant cultural scene.
These are just a few of the many capital cities in Europe, each with its own unique charm and history.
