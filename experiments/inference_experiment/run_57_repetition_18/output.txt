Climate change is caused by an increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, which trap heat from the sun. The main source of greenhouse gas emissions is the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas. This process releases large amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, contributing to global warming.

However, other gases such as methane and nitrous oxide also play significant roles in climate change. Nitrous oxide is emitted mainly through agricultural practices like fertilization and animal manure management.

The energy industry is another major emitter, with coal-fired power plants being the primary source of greenhouse gases in many countries. Agriculture is also a significant source of methane and nitrous oxide emissions.
