 Here's a list of capitals for most European countries, with some exceptions where the country consists of multiple regions or territories:

1. Albania - Tirana
2. Andorra - Andorra la Vella
3. Armenia - Yerevan
4. Austria - Vienna (Wien)
5. Azerbaijan - Baku
6. Belarus - Minsk
7. Belgium - Brussels (Brussel)
8. Bosnia and Herzegovina - Sarajevo (Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina), Banja Luka (Republica Srpska), Mostar (Canton 10)
9. Croatia - Zagreb
10. Cyprus - Nicosia (Lefkosia)
11. Czech Republic - Prague (Praha)
12. Denmark - Copenhagen (København)
13. Estonia - Tallinn
14. Finland - Helsinki (Helsingfors)
15. France - Paris
16. Georgia - Tbilisi
17. Germany - Berlin
18. Greece - Athens (Athina)
19. Hungary - Budapest
20. Iceland - Reykjavik
21. Ireland - Dublin
22. Italy - Rome (Roma)
23. Kazakhstan (border with Europe, but primarily in Asia) - Nur-Sultan (Astana until 2019)
24. Latvia - Riga
25. Liechtenstein - Vaduz
26. Lithuania - Vilnius
27. Luxembourg - City of Luxembourg (Luxemburg Ville)
28. Macedonia - Skopje (Skopje/Štip for UNMIK Kosovo)
29. Malta - Valletta
30. Moldova - Chișinău
31. Monaco - Monaco-Ville (Monte Carlo is not a separate city but a ward within the Principality of Monaco)
32. Montenegro - Podgorica
33. Netherlands - Amsterdam
34. North Macedonia - Skopje
35. Norway - Oslo
36. Poland - Warsaw (Warszawa)
37. Portugal - Lisbon (Lisboa)
38. Romania - Bucharest (București)
39. Russia (partially in Europe, primarily in Asia) - Moscow (Moskva)
40. San Marino - City of San Marino (San Marino Citta)
41. Serbia - Belgrade (Beograd)
42. Slovakia - Bratislava
43. Slovenia - Ljubljana
44. Spain - Madrid
45. Sweden - Stockholm
46. Switzerland - Bern (Berna) or Bern (Berne in German-speaking regions)
47. Turkey (border with Europe, primarily in Asia) - Ankara
48. Ukraine - Kiev (Kyiv)
49. United Kingdom - London
50. Vatican City - Vatican City (Stato della Città del Vaticano)

This list covers all the European countries recognized by the United Nations, excluding the disputed territory of Kosovo, which officially declared its capital as Priština but is not universally recognized as a sovereign state. The capitals for territories such as the Faroe Islands (Tórshavn) and Gibraltar (Gibraltar) are not included since they are not considered European countries by some sources.
