 The capital city of Europe is Brussels, Belgium, which serves as both the seat of the European Union and the headquarters of the Council of the European Union. However, there are several other major cities throughout Europe that have been designated as capitals for various countries or regions.

The most common capital in Europe is Vienna, Austria, which is not only the capital city of Austria but also serves as the seat of the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) and the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO). Berlin, Germany, is another major capital, serving as the capital of both Germany and the Federal Republic of Germany.

Paris, France, is another important European capital city, serving as the capital of France and one of the largest cities in Europe. Madrid, Spain, is the capital of Spain and one of the most populous cities in Europe. London, England, is a major European capital and serves as the seat of the United Kingdom's government.

In addition to these cities, there are many other important capitals throughout Europe, such as Rome, Italy; Budapest, Hungary; Athens, Greece; Warsaw, Poland; Lisbon, Portugal; Riga, Latvia; Oslo, Norway; Tallinn, Estonia; and The Hague, the capital of the Netherlands.

Each of these European capital cities has its own unique history, culture, and significance. Some are known for their art and architecture, while others are centers of political power or economic activity. Regardless of their individual characteristics, all of Europe's major cities play a crucial role in shaping the continent's social, cultural, and economic landscape.
