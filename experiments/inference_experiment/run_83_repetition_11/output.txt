This paper provides a comprehensive overview of artificial intelligence (AI), encompassing its definition, applications, history, and societal impact. 

Key insights include: AI's evolution from research domain to mainstream integration, evident in everyday applications like search engines and recommendation systems.  The paper highlights the diverse subfields within AI, focusing on goals like reasoning, learning, and natural language processing. It traces AI's history through periods of both enthusiasm and disillusionment, culminating in the recent "AI boom" fueled by advancements in deep learning and transformer architecture. 

Finally, the paper acknowledges the growing concerns surrounding AI's unintended consequences and potential risks, emphasizing the need for regulatory frameworks to ensure responsible development and deployment.
