Here are the capitals of all European countries, according to widely accepted definitions:

**Western Europe:**

* Andorra: Andorra la Vella
* Austria: Vienna
* Belgium: Brussels
* France: Paris
* Germany: Berlin
* Ireland: Dublin
* Liechtenstein: Vaduz
* Luxembourg: Luxembourg City
* Monaco: Monaco
* Netherlands: Amsterdam
* Switzerland: Bern
* United Kingdom: London

**Eastern Europe:**

* Belarus: Minsk
* Bulgaria: Sofia
* Czech Republic: Prague
* Hungary: Budapest
* Moldova: Chișinău
* Poland: Warsaw
* Romania: Bucharest
* Russia: Moscow (Note: Russia spans both Europe and Asia)
* Slovakia: Bratislava
* Ukraine: Kyiv


**Northern Europe:**

* Denmark: Copenhagen
* Estonia: Tallinn
* Finland: Helsinki
* Iceland: Reykjavik
* Latvia: Riga
* Lithuania: Vilnius
* Norway: Oslo
* Sweden: Stockholm

**Southern Europe:**

* Albania: Tirana
* Bosnia and Herzegovina: Sarajevo
* Croatia: Zagreb
* Greece: Athens
* Italy: Rome
* Kosovo: Pristina 
* Macedonia (North): Skopje
* Malta: Valletta
* Montenegro: Podgorica
* Portugal: Lisbon
* San Marino: San Marino
* Serbia: Belgrade
* Slovenia: Ljubljana
* Spain: Madrid
* Vatican City: Vatican City


**Note:**

* The inclusion of some countries, like Russia and Turkey, can be debated as they span multiple continents. 
* This list reflects generally accepted political boundaries and might change due to geopolitical shifts.
