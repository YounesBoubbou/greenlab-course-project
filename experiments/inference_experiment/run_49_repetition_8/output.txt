The Industrial Revolution's legacy of increased greenhouse gas emissions has catalyzed significant economic and technological advancements aimed at mitigating its impacts. Notable among these are the development and adoption of renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power. Technological innovations like photovoltaic cells, wind turbines, and battery storage systems have become integral to modern energy infrastructure, offering sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels.

Economically, these advancements have spurred growth in green industries, creating jobs and stimulating innovation. For instance, the global renewable energy sector employed over 12 million people as of 2020, according to the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA). This growth is driven by both government incentives and private investments, reflecting a broader shift towards sustainable development.

Moreover, technological progress in carbon capture and storage (CCS) technologies has also emerged as a key response. While still in the early stages of widespread implementation, CCS technologies hold promise for reducing emissions in industries that are difficult to decarbonize.

In addition, smart grid technology has improved the efficiency of energy distribution systems by enabling better management of supply and demand. This technology can integrate renewable energy sources more effectively, enhancing the reliability and resilience of power networks while reducing overall carbon footprints.

Finally, international cooperation through agreements like the Paris Agreement plays a crucial role in fostering these advancements. Such frameworks not only set ambitious climate goals but also facilitate knowledge sharing, collaboration on research and development, and financial support for developing countries to transition to low-carbon economies.

Overall, these economic and technological responses demonstrate humanity's evolving approach to addressing climate change, moving from reactive adaptation to proactive mitigation and sustainable development strategies.
