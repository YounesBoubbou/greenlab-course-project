Fossil fuel combustion is the largest contributor to CO2 emissions, accounting for 71% of the global total. Transportation accounts for 20-30% of global energy-related emissions.

Industries like power generation, manufacturing, and transportation are significant sources of greenhouse gas emissions. In the power generation sector, coal burning accounts for a significant portion of emissions. Manufacturing industries contribute to emissions through the use of chemicals, transportation of goods, and energy consumption. The transportation industry is one of the largest emitters of CO2, with road transport being the most significant contributor.

The agriculture industry contributes to greenhouse gas emissions through livestock farming and fertilizers used in crop production. Waste management practices such as landfilling contribute to methane emissions, which is a potent greenhouse gas that has 28 times more warming potential than carbon dioxide. 

In summary, human activities are responsible for the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, contributing to climate change. The transportation industry and energy production sectors account for the majority of emissions, while agriculture and waste management also play significant roles. Efforts to reduce emissions from these sectors will be critical to mitigating the impacts of climate change.
